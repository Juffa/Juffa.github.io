Gargamel (Smurfs)

So I wanted to try to make a character, one I would rig so I could move it around into different poses... Gargamel was my first attempt.

I did this just by moving and extruding points, just like one would do a retopology on a sculpted surface, but without a surface.
I wasn't confident I could do any scupting, it felt daunting.
The Gargamel ended up looking about right, but the rigging was a problem...
In the tutorial I had watched, they manually set the weights for vertices, but it just took a lot of time and didn't look that good at all.
Also none of the the things that aren't part of the main mesh weren't following the rig, even if it somehow worked in the tutorial video?

Later I found out a lot easier way to do that and also the proper way to rig clothes and accessories.

Vizlet

Vizlet is a character from a webcomic Valsalia.

So I had done those '1 hour sculpt' practices for a while, and wanted to spend a little more time into a new character.
This one, or rather the character's basic mesh, is completely sculpted.
It started as just a head:

I had been watching some blender videos on youtube by YanSculpts, and decided to use same or similar methods he was using...
Or at least try to. He makes amazing things in Blender.
Then I expanded it into a full body mesh:

Then I made her some clothes and added some basic materials, they actually look good enough, though she's still missing some pink hue from the face and ears.
At this point I really wanted to make her move, so I looked again into the rigging process and found a lot better advice than the previous tutorials I watched... That was just to use automatic weights instead of manually setting the weights. It works perfectly if the skeleton is well defined.
Also the rigging clothes problem was somewhat solved, I got them to follow the mesh, but it obviously needs probably shape keys or bones for the clothes, too.
The mesh is probably too highpoly and my computer is way too old and weak for this, since moving the character now is slow like 1 frame per second, or less...
But I still managed to make her sit and grab a mug. :)

One hour sculpts

Quick one hour sculpting practices.

Some of these are made with using random reference images, probably the better looking ones..
The idea here is to sculpt for one hour, no more, no less, and after one hour the sculpt cannot be continued.
If I want to make one into a full-blown character, I'd have to start from scratch. That's a self-imposed rule that helps with learning things.
