A leather couch

A leather couch I have in my livingroom.

The real life couch is not in pristine condition like the 3d version, since it's rather old and been through a lot.
I wanted to model something a bit more challenging...
Also this is the first project where I did some sculpting, it felt really difficult.
I sculpted the grooves and stuff like that, baked a normal map out of them and now they are applied on top of the clean lowpoly mesh.

Gargamel (Smurfs)

So I wanted to try to make a character, one I would rig so I could move it around into different poses... Gargamel was my first attempt.

I did this just by moving and extruding points, just like one would do a retopology on a sculpted surface, but without a surface.
I wasn't confident I could do any scupting, it felt daunting.
The Gargamel ended up looking about right, but the rigging was a problem...
In the tutorial I had watched, they manually set the weights for vertices, but it just took a lot of time and didn't look that good at all.
Also none of the the things that aren't part of the main mesh weren't following the rig, even if it somehow worked in the tutorial video?

Later I found out a lot easier way to do that and also the proper way to rig clothes and accessories.

A Wacom pen

I decided to model something I have on my desk. So I modeled a Wacom pen.

The whole pen has a roughness map on it, but it's really visible on the button. Looks like fingerprints, just like on the real thing.
Proper textures and various maps really make the difference here.
It turned out VERY realistic, but it is a 3d image I assure you!

A Wacom tablet

Yeah Ok I felt it was necessary to model the tablet too, after the pen.

This tablet was a lot more difficult to make than the pen, it's actually a single mesh (except for the lights and so, they are separate meshes). It's not 100% accurate, but close enough.
